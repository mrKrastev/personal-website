I trained karate for 12 years. This was the first sport/martial art that I trained. I was 5 when my father enrolled me and I am easily say that this was life changing experience. I used to have astma and thanks to the karate exercises I managed to overcome this condition. Moreover, karate helped me with building my character. It taught me patience, discipline and self-control. Karate was also the first activity that made me realize how important is to reflect on mistakes and always try to imrpove. I started competing when I was 7 and for 2 years I didn't have any success on any of the competitions. However, I kept attending the lessons and refused to leave defeated. And of course, hard work paid off and I managed to win my first competition... and not only. I kept the first place on every competition for 5 years in a row. I reached up to brown belt (1 level below black) and then I moved in UK.

We had less and less sparrings and I decided to find another sport that satisfies me. This is how I started boxing. I enjoy every session as I have a lot of energy and I always have the oppurtunity to release the stress. It also greatly improves cardio and physique which is a good bonus. Overall, I find it a great way to spend my time after a long day at University.
